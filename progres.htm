is hitting you
(polite form)		 - - - - gur bualadh builley shiu
  • The Manx' forms (Ta eh builley eh = he is hitting him) fall out of the pattern because the personal pronoun is simply hung onto the end.
    (also not fitting is the spelling: in trad. Irish spelling it would be: "*Tá é buileadh é")
  • the Connacht- (Conamara-) form of the preposition do is pronounced [ge]
  • in older writings, there os the form agá, also 'ghá for ag + a (today: á)
