In Munster, in place of déan, the regular verb déin or dein is used (in the future/conditional, still déan).
In Ulster, there are absolute forms in the present ním, níonn sé (from do-ghníonn, see below)

Older forms

Once there was an absolute form in the present: (do-)ghním, (do-)ghníonn sé and the imperfect: (do-)ghnínn, (do)ghníodh sé.
Déanann sé, etc. was then only the dependent form.
In the future/conditional, (do-)ghéanaidh sé became absolute and déanfaidh sé dependent.:
