various stems in the present, imperative and imperfect (stem té-) in the preterite (stem chuaigh), future and conditional (stem rach-), special dependent form in the preterite (stem deach-)

In place of téigh in the imperative 2nd person, téir or téirigh is mostly in use.

In Connemara, future-/conditional forms with gabh- are common, as well as the imperative forms: gabh!, gabhigí!
(possibly through the engl. "go", gabh actually means "take")
here then the verbal noun goil, verbal adjective: goite
